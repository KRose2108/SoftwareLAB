Chess Pieces

Chess pieces consist of six different types: King, Queen, Rooks, Knights, Bishops, and Pawns. Each piece has its unique way of moving on the board. The goal of the game is to checkmate the opponent's King while protecting your own King.

Chess Openings

Midgame Strategies

During the midgame, players aim to create threats, control the board, and look for tactical opportunities to win material or gain positional advantage. Key concepts in the midgame include pawn structure, piece coordination, and tactical motifs like forks, pins, and skewers.

Endgame Strategies

The endgame begins when there are fewer pieces on the board. Players focus on promoting pawns to queens and checkmating the opponent. Proper King and pawn endgame techniques, along with understanding basic checkmating patterns, are crucial to win in the endgame.
